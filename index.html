About Me

I'm Ada, a Data Scientist with a passion for uncovering insights from data and making data-driven decisions. Currently, I'm a Data Scientist at the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, where I do research on large language models. I'm also a calligrapher and classical violinist. Welcome to my personal website!

Why I Chose Data Science

Inspiration from John Tukey 😃

John Tukey once said, “The best thing about being a statistician is that you get to play in everyone's backyard.” This sentiment resonated deeply with me and was a major reason why I pursued statistics, which naturally led me to data science. It’s applicable to nearly any field!

A Perfect Blend of Programming and Statistics 💻

  • In high school, A.P. Statistics was my favorite class.
  • In college, I enjoyed my coding classes and the logical problem-solving they required.
  • Data science is the perfect combination of these two subjects, bringing the best of both worlds together!

Data Science Aligns with my Personality 💁🏻‍♀️

  • Love for Organization — Data scientists need to organize their data first to make sense of it! My friends tell me "you're the most organized person I know."
  • Data-Driven Decision-Making — Every decision I make is rooted in logic and evidence, which is the heart of data science!
  • Attention to Detail — Just like a data scientist should validate their results, I naturally double-check everything, ensuring accuracy and reliability in my work.

Fun Facts About "Ada"

It’s a Technical Name 👩🏻‍💻

  • Ada is a technical name! This is a coincidence. When my parents named me, they had no idea I would go into artificial intelligence!
  • Ada Lovelace was the world’s first computer scientist.
  • There’s even a programming language named Ada.

It Rhymes with “Data” 😆

It’s pronounced “dayduh”, not “dadduh”. :)

It’s Pronounced like Eta η

Ada is pronounced like the Greek letter eta η, which is the learning rate in the gradient descent algorithm. I love this connection, because I LOVE to learn (as a child, I’d count down the days until school started)!

There is another, more advanced version of the vanilla gradient descent algorithm, called Adaptive Gradient, or AdaGrad. Instead of a fixed learning rate, the learning rate adjusts, much like people do in real life. I love how machine learning algorithms reflect human learning.
